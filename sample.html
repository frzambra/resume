Experience

Center for Advanced Land Management Information Technologies (CALMIT), Lincoln, EE.UU.
Research Internship 2016 I came to CALMIT to improve my research about agricultural drought using remote sensing data and derived drought indices. Currently I have been working in a reserach article about satellite long-term data of precipitation, products CHRIPS 2.0 and Persiann-CDR to evaluate its accuracy for use in Chile. In April I will start other article with the aim of evaluate agricultural drought in Chile using different remote sensing drought indices as vegetation, land surface temperature and precipitation.
National Institute of Agricultural Research (INIA), Chile
Consultant Researcher 2012-2015 As part of a team for the managing of agroclimatic risk, I proposed, derived and assess an agricultural drought index to be applied in Chile for agro-drought monitoring. Since the year 2015 is used for the ministry of agriculture as a tool to help stakeholder to improve the take of decision when faced a drought period.
National Irrigation Commission (CNR), Chile
Consultant Researcher 2010-2011 I resolved the problem on how the water rights of farmers are linked. I developed the algorithms to perform the network analysis and visualization of farmers water rights data-base for an important watershed in Chile usign `IGraph’ theory and the R programming tool. This was very useful to understand how the water rights are connected to each others and allow to identify important problems in its historical transactions, an unprecedent work in Chile.
General Water Directorate (DGA), Chile
Engineer 2011 Perform fiscalization in accordance with the authority granted by the water law to the water resources management institution of Chile.
